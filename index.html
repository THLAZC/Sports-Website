HISTORY OF THE ATLANTA HAWKS

  • The Atlanta Hawks, first known as the Tri-Cities Blackhawks, were founded in 1946.
  • Originally part of the National Basketball League (NBL), they joined the NBA in 1949 as part of the merger between the NBL and the Basketball Association of America (BAA).
  • In 1951, the then known Blackhawks relocated to Milwaukee while also shortening their name to the Hawks.
  • The team relocated again to St. Louis in 1955 before finally moving to Atlanta in 1968 where the team has resided ever since.
  • Their first and only championship came in 1958 when the S. Louis Hawks defeated the Boston Celtics in six games.
  • They presently reside in the State Farm Arena with their current owner being Tony Ressler.
